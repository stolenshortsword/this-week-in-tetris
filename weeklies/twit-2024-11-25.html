Intro

We're always looking for new contributors, so if you're interested, join our Discord server and say hello. Huge thank you to sonic, who wrote two of the features and provided the Challengers scores for the month. The server also features the Almost-Daily Update, which provides the scores of yesterday's big matches and lets you know what's going on in the future.

The numbers next to the players are their November Premiere Poll rankings, not their tournament seeds. Scores in the tables are given in thousands; an (i) or (a) next to a score indicates that the topout was intentional or aggressive, respectively. As always, let me know if you found this entertaining or useful, or if there's anything else you'd like to see.

I went into CTL thinking both subdivisions would have two Div. 2 promotees that the rest of the field would beat up on in sweeps. TetrisTime is the one in 1A that has proved that wrong, with blazing fast improvement with rolling now that he’s left table tapping behind, and after great looking losses to meme and dog, he’s finally got his big win. He reverse swept Coalbucket, taking advantage of Coal’s inability to find his footing on the killscreen with a trio of a 1.1, 1.2 and 1.3 in the last two games and showing real familiarity the post-post transition. Dog picks up a huge win over Myles in his first since CTWC (after losing to him in CTM July and Lone Star) despite back-to-back rollovers by the Great One (before a 15 line ET…). Meme somehow maintains a perfect 15-0 record with a sweep over SV, who actually played great. -stolenshortsword

Classic Tetris Monthly

November Masters Orange Bracket

There’s currently a lot of movement going on in the Tetris world as we enter the offseason, so to speak. A lot of players are getting busy at school and/or need a break. Meanwhile, there are other players who’ve risen meteorically to the top; this gave room for a lot of upsets and unexpected names appearing. Two of those players who’ve risen to prominence, of course, are meme and TetrisTime. TetrisTime had a much better showing in CTL than in these matches, but even then, meme’s improvement has been so rapid recently that she was the clear favorite. No surprises here; TetrisTime simply couldn’t get much killscreen play going and topped out rather early on 29 both games. Nek0 is another player who’s risen to the top recently, bursting onto the scene with an incredibly strong August performance and sticking around since. Neko versus Myles was one of the longer matches in the early rounds this month, and an upset win for the former. Myles took the first game with a rollover and a level 51, but Neko won via chasedowns in the two games after in a reverse sweep, taking Myles out to many people’s surprise. Neko’s run, however, then ended swiftly at the hands of meme; he couldn’t get into the same groove that took him past level 50 and beyond in the previous match, and meme was able to outlast him both games. As a whole, the best of three format adopted for these early rounds was quite brutal, and turned No Cap November into a much deadlier format than I had anticipated. At the time of writing, we’ve seen both semifinals of No Cap, and it definitely made a huge difference in terms of results, so it remains unknown what the optimal format for such a competition should have been. -sonic CTM November 2024 Masters Event Round 1 (Orange), November 17, 2024
